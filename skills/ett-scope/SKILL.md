## Mission

ETT is an independent Danish think tank working for a strong, competitive Danish business sector as the foundation for a prosperous society. Core framing: Denmark is a small, open economy increasingly shaped by external forces — geopolitical tensions, technological breakthroughs, shifts in global trade. This sharpens the requirements for both competitiveness and resilience.

Output format: independent analysis translated into **actionable insights and concrete recommendations** for business and policymakers. Not academic for its own sake — always ends in a "so what" for Danish business or policy.

## Audience

Business leaders, government authorities, politicians, and research communities. Analysis must be accessible to a non-technical decision-maker while being methodologically credible to an expert reader.

## Focus areas

**Rammevilkår og investeringer** — How do regulation, taxation, capital access, and geopolitical shifts affect Danish business competitiveness? Relevant: corporate tax incidence, investment barriers, EU regulatory burden, capital market access for Danish firms.

**Energi og forsyningssikkerhed** — How is access to stable, competitive, and green energy secured? Relevant: Danish electricity market (DK1/DK2, Nord Pool), RE intermittency and price volatility, nuclear vs. offshore wind system costs, energy system modelling (EnergyPLAN), sector coupling, interconnector capacity. *Linus's primary quantitative domain at ETT.*

**Teknologi og digital suverænitet** — How is access to critical technology, data, and compute secured in a world of technological rivalry? Relevant: semiconductor supply chains, cloud dependency, AI infrastructure, EU tech policy.

**Ejerskab og arbejdskraft** — How is Danish ownership, talent, and key functions retained in strategically significant firms? Relevant: foreign acquisition of Danish companies, talent retention, labour market flexibility, human capital in intangible-intensive sectors.

**Forsvar og sikkerhed** — How is Danish defence industry and dual-use capacity developed as threat landscapes shift and defence spending rises? Relevant: Danish defence procurement, NATO obligations, dual-use industrial policy.

**Ledelse og governance** — How is firms' strategic capacity strengthened at the intersection of market, technology, and geopolitics? Relevant: board composition, scenario planning, geopolitical risk management for Danish firms.

## Analytical style

- Frame around Danish competitiveness or resilience — not general welfare economics
- Reference scenario is always Denmark or Danish firms, not a generic EU or OECD country
- Policy recommendations should be concrete and directed at a named actor (government, Folketing, specific ministry, industry association)
- Quantitative work is published alongside qualitative framing — numbers need a narrative
- ETT is not partisan, but it represents the business sector's perspective: efficiency and competitiveness arguments carry weight; distributional arguments are secondary unless directly relevant

## Extending an analysis — natural directions

When asked to extend ETT work, consider:
- **Cross-pillar linkages**: e.g. energy price volatility (Energi) → investment incentives (Rammevilkår) → foreign ownership (Ejerskab)
- **Scenario variants**: what does the conclusion look like under different policy assumptions (nuclear vs. no nuclear, high vs. low carbon price)?
- **EU/international comparison**: how does Denmark compare to peers on the dimension being studied?
- **Stakeholder translation**: who acts on this finding, and what specifically do they decide?
